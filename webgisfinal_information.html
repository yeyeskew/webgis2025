Study Information

We chose to study the 10 metro systems in North America with the highest annual ridership numbers. We then mapped these metro systems with ArcGIS Online.

In our study, we determined that dividing the average annual ridership by system length in kilometers to keep it internationally standard. We gathered data from city amd university level research for our metrics for population, ridership, and our shapefiles.

This formula comes out to be AR (Annual Ridership) / SL (System Length in kilometers) = EC (Efficiency Coefficient).

The higher the EC of a city, the higher the ridership per rail kilometer. This gives a rough estimate of how well utilized a system is instead of looking at the raw numbers of a system and comparing those.

While there are many other variables that contribute to ridership (such as metro area population), those were not taken into consideration because a larger metro population will automatically correlate to some higher level of ridership over a smaller city.
